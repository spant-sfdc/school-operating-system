# Content Collection Guide

**For school staff — not developers.** This explains how to gather the photos, text, and documents the website needs, in plain terms, without needing to know anything about how the website is built. If you're looking for the list of exactly _what's_ needed, see [TEXT_REGISTRY.md](./TEXT_REGISTRY.md) and [IMAGE_REGISTRY.md](./IMAGE_REGISTRY.md) instead — this document is about _how_ to gather it well.

---

## Before You Start

- **Nothing here needs to be perfect.** Good, honest, well-lit photos beat professional-studio photos that take months to arrange. Send what you have; we can always improve it later.
- **Every photo should show something real.** Don't stage a classroom to look like a different school's brochure — the actual classroom, the actual playground, photographed as they actually are, is what makes the website trustworthy.
- **When in doubt about wording (a policy, a rule, a claim), ask before guessing.** The website will not publish anything that isn't confirmed — see [TEXT_REGISTRY.md](./TEXT_REGISTRY.md)'s "Replacement Notes" column for exactly what each piece of text needs to say.

## Photography

### General best practices (apply to everything below)

- **Shoot in good daylight where possible** — near windows, or outdoors, rather than under fluorescent lighting alone.
- **Horizontal (landscape) orientation** for most shots — it fits the website's layout better than vertical phone photos.
- **Take more than you need.** For each item below, 5–10 shots gives us room to pick the best one, instead of being stuck with the only photo taken.
- **Ask permission before photographing any student's face directly and identifiably**, and check with school leadership on the school's policy for using student photos publicly before sending them.
- **A recent smartphone camera is genuinely good enough.** No special equipment is required for a first pass.

### Classrooms

_What the website needs (see [IMG-005](./IMAGE_REGISTRY.md)):_ one or two photos that represent a typical classroom.

- Photograph a classroom that's in its normal, in-use state — not artificially tidied to the point it looks unused.
- Include some students or a teacher in the shot if possible (with permission) — an empty room reads as less alive than one with people learning in it.
- Capture the room from a corner, angled to show depth (desks, board, windows), rather than a flat shot of one wall.

### Principal's Portrait

_What the website needs (see [IMG-004](./IMAGE_REGISTRY.md)):_ one professional-quality headshot or half-body portrait of the Principal.

- Plain or softly blurred background — a school corridor or office is fine; avoid busy/cluttered backgrounds.
- Good, even lighting on the face — avoid strong overhead light that casts shadows under the eyes.
- A warm, approachable expression fits this platform's voice better than a stiff, formal pose.

### Campus (Building & Grounds)

_What the website needs (see [IMG-005 through IMG-008](./IMAGE_REGISTRY.md)):_ photos representing the campus as a whole — building exterior, playground, and general grounds.

- An exterior shot of the main building entrance, taken on a clear day.
- The playground/outdoor play area, ideally while genuinely in use.
- Any distinctive feature of the campus worth showing (a garden, a covered walkway, an assembly area).

### Library

_What the website needs (see [IMG-006](./IMAGE_REGISTRY.md)):_ a photo of the library/reading space.

- Show the room's actual scale and shelving, not a single close-up of a few books.
- If students are commonly seen reading there, a candid (permitted) shot of that is more useful than an empty room.

### School Events

_What the website needs (see [IMG-008](./IMAGE_REGISTRY.md)):_ photos from a recent school event (sports day, annual function, cultural program, etc.).

- Wide shots that show scale and energy, plus one or two closer shots of specific activities.
- Recent events are much better than old ones — a five-year-old event photo can make a website feel out of date.

## Documents

_What the website needs (see [DOCUMENT_REGISTRY.md](./DOCUMENT_REGISTRY.md)):_ 13 documents across four categories — admission forms, academic documents, mandatory public disclosures, and school policies, all listed on `/documents`.

- **Send PDFs, not scanned photos of paper where avoidable.** A proper digital PDF (even a simple one made from a Word document) looks and prints far better than a photographed page.
- **Affiliation-dependent documents first.** The Mandatory Public Disclosures category (`DOC-007`–`DOC-010`) can't be finalized until `TXT-001` (the school's affiliation/recognition status) is confirmed — start there if you're prioritizing.
- **Use the school's real letterhead/branding on official documents** (the Fee Structure, policies, certificates) — these are documents families may print and keep, not just web copy.
- **If a policy genuinely doesn't exist yet** (no formal Anti-Ragging Policy has been written, for instance), say so plainly rather than sending something informal labeled as if it were the final policy — the website will note it's pending rather than publish an unofficial document as if it were official.
- **Filenames don't need to match anything specific** — the website tracks each document by what it _is_ (`DOC-001`, "Admission Form — Nursery to UKG"), not by a particular filename; send the file however it's natively named.

## Logos

_What the website needs (see [IMG-002](./IMAGE_REGISTRY.md)):_ the school's logo, if one exists.

- The highest-resolution version available — a small, blurry logo file cannot be improved after the fact.
- A version with a transparent background (PNG format) if possible — this lets the logo work on both light and dark backgrounds, since the website supports both.
- If the school does not yet have a formal logo, that's fine — say so, rather than sending a placeholder; the website currently displays the school's name as text and works correctly without a logo.

## Videos

**Nothing is currently required here** — see [VIDEO_REGISTRY.md](./VIDEO_REGISTRY.md). If the school wants to provide video content in the future (a Principal's welcome message, a campus tour), general best practice guidance:

- Horizontal orientation, at least 1080p resolution if the recording device supports it.
- Keep it reasonably short (under 2 minutes for a welcome message) — website visitors rarely watch long embedded videos to the end.
- Good, clear audio matters more than production polish — a video with poor sound is harder to watch than one with simple visuals and clear audio.

## Text and Policy Information

Most of what's needed isn't a photo — it's a confirmed answer to a specific question (an age cutoff, an office hour, an FAQ answer). [TEXT_REGISTRY.md](./TEXT_REGISTRY.md) lists every one of these individually, with exactly which page it appears on. When answering:

- **Be specific.** "Office hours: 8am–2pm, Monday–Saturday" is usable; "school hours vary" is not.
- **If a policy genuinely doesn't exist yet** (e.g., there's no formal entrance-test policy), say that plainly — the website will state it honestly rather than invent a policy that doesn't exist.
- **If the same fact is asked for in two places** (the registry flags these with a ⚠), one answer covers both — you only need to confirm it once.

## What Happens After You Send Something

Every item moves through the same stages: **Required → Requested → Received → Approved → Optimized → Published.** "Received" just means it's arrived and is being reviewed — it isn't live yet. "Approved" means the school has confirmed it's accurate. "Optimized" is a technical step (resizing/compressing an image, for example) done before it goes live. Nothing skips a stage.
