# Database Review

**Purpose:** Entity-by-entity physical review of the 11 entities named in this task's brief, plus the four questions the task asked directly. Field-level shape comes from [docs/domain/DATABASE_SCHEMA.md](../domain/DATABASE_SCHEMA.md) and is not restated here except where a physical concern requires citing a specific field.

---

## 1. The Central Principle: Identity vs. Fact

Before the entity-by-entity review, one distinction governs most of what follows, so it's worth stating once instead of re-deriving it 11 times:

- **`Student`, `Teacher`, `Guardian` are durable identity.** They exist once, persist for the person's entire relationship with the school (and, for `Student`, beyond it — a former student's record must remain producible for a TC reissue a decade later).
- **`Enrollment`, `AttendanceRecord`, `MarksRecord`, `PromotionRecord`, `TransferCertificate` are temporal facts.** Each is true about a specific person in a specific academic year, and — critically — a person can have more than one of these facts pointing at overlapping identity (a detained student has two `Enrollment`s in the same `Class`, in different `AcademicYear`s).

Every "why does X reference Enrollment instead of Student" answer in § 9 below is this same principle applied to a specific pair of tables. Getting this distinction right physically (not just conceptually, where [D-022](../DECISIONS.md#d-022--school-domain-model-board-agnostic-enrollment-centric-rte-aware) already got it right) is the single highest-leverage decision in this entire review.

## 2. Student

| Dimension               | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary key             | `cuid2` (Prisma default) — insert volume is low (hundreds/year per school), B-tree locality doesn't matter here the way it does for `AttendanceRecord`. No need for a time-ordered ID.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Unique constraints      | `(schoolId, admissionNumber)` — **not** globally unique; two schools will independently issue "STU-001". `udisePen` globally unique when present (it's a national identifier, correctly cross-school).                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Indexes                 | `(schoolId, status)` — nearly every Admin query implicitly filters to `status = ACTIVE`; without this index, that filter degrades to a sequential scan the moment enrollment crosses a few thousand rows.                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Composite indexes       | `(schoolId, status, admissionNumber)` if admission-number lookup-by-partial-match becomes a real UX need; not needed for V1's exact-match lookups.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Lookup tables           | None needed. `category` stays a plain `String` (see [ENUM_STRATEGY.md § 2](./ENUM_STRATEGY.md#2-fields-that-should-not-be-native-enums)) — the admission-category list is school-configurable, not a fixed reference set worth a table.                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Enums                   | `status` (`ACTIVE`/`ALUMNI`/`TRANSFERRED_OUT`/`WITHDRAWN`) — good native-enum candidate: fixed, universal, not Admin-editable.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Audit fields            | Full `AuditLog` coverage on every mutation — a student record is a legal/administrative document; name corrections and category changes must be traceable (already stated in [DOMAIN_MODEL.md § 4.2](../domain/DOMAIN_MODEL.md#42-student), confirmed here as a hard requirement, not optional).                                                                                                                            |
| Soft delete             | Yes — `deletedAt` is wrong here; use `status` instead (already a lifecycle field). A student is never truly "deleted," only moved to `ALUMNI`/`TRANSFERRED_OUT`/`WITHDRAWN`. Adding a _second_ delete mechanism (`deletedAt`) alongside `status` would create two competing "is this student gone" answers — pick one. **Recommendation: no `deletedAt` field on `Student` at all; `status` is the only lifecycle signal.** |
| History                 | Carried entirely by `Enrollment` (one row per year) — `Student` itself holds no year-scoped history.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Archive strategy        | None needed at V1 scale (a few thousand rows after 15 years for one school). Revisit only at real multi-tenant scale (Epic H), and even then, `ALUMNI` students are cheap to keep live — the archival pressure is on `AttendanceRecord`/`AuditLog`, not `Student`.                                                                                                                                                          |
| Reporting impact        | Source for Enrollment/School Strength report ([REPORTING_MODEL.md § 4](../domain/REPORTING_MODEL.md#4-operational-reports)); `status` is the primary filter dimension for nearly every Admin report.                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Expected query patterns | (a) lookup by `admissionNumber` within a school, (b) list active students by class/section (via `Enrollment` join), (c) name search (see § 10, flagged as a future need, not a V1 index).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Expected growth         | 200–2,000 students per school ([PRODUCT_VISION.md § 3](../PRODUCT_VISION.md#3-target-audience)), single-digit-percent annual churn. Trivial row count even at 15-year, multi-hundred-school scale — `Student` is never the table that drives a partitioning or archival decision.                                                                                                                                           |

## 3. Guardian

| Dimension               | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary key             | `cuid2`.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Unique constraints      | None at the `Guardian` level — two different guardian records can legitimately share a phone number (e.g., a shared landline was common historically; even a shared mobile between spouses isn't unheard of) or the same guardian could theoretically be entered twice by different office staff before being merged. Don't force uniqueness where the real world doesn't guarantee it.               |
| Indexes                 | `(phone)` — **the single most important index on this table.** The front-desk workflow ("a parent calls, staff looks them up by phone") is a real, frequent operation this domain model didn't explicitly name as a query pattern but is implied by `Contact`'s own emphasis on phone as the primary channel — worth stating explicitly here since it's easy to miss without a dedicated review pass. |
| Composite indexes       | None needed — `StudentGuardian` (the join table) carries the composite lookups (see below).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Lookup tables           | None.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Enums                   | `StudentGuardian.relationshipType` (`FATHER`/`MOTHER`/`LEGAL_GUARDIAN`/`OTHER`) — good native-enum candidate on the join table, not `Guardian` itself.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Audit fields            | Contact-detail changes should be traceable (reliability of emergency contact matters) — standard `AuditLog` coverage, not elevated beyond that.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Soft delete             | Yes, `deletedAt` — a guardian relationship can end (custody change, record correction) without deleting a person who may still be linked to a sibling.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| History                 | None beyond the audit trail — `Guardian` has no year-scoped facts of its own.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| Archive strategy        | None needed — same reasoning as `Student`.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Reporting impact        | Minimal direct reporting use; feeds TC generation (guardian contact on record) and future Parent Portal auth (not V1).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Expected query patterns | (a) lookup by phone, (b) list guardians for a student (via `StudentGuardian`, `studentId`-scoped), (c) list students for a guardian (via `StudentGuardian`, `guardianId`-scoped — needed the moment a guardian has more than one child at the school).                                                                                                                                                |
| Expected growth         | Roughly 1.2–1.8× `Student` count (siblings share guardians; not every student has two distinct guardian rows). Same low-volume profile as `Student`.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |

**`StudentGuardian` (join table) specifics:** composite unique `(studentId, guardianId)` already correct in [DATABASE_SCHEMA.md § 4](../domain/DATABASE_SCHEMA.md#4-people--identity). Add index `(guardianId)` explicitly — the existing unique's leading column is `studentId`, so "all children of this guardian" would not use that index efficiently without a second one leading with `guardianId`.

## 4. Academic Year

| Dimension               | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary key             | `cuid2` — trivial insert volume (one row per school per year).                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Unique constraints      | `(schoolId, label)`.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Indexes                 | `(schoolId, isCurrent)` — every request in the entire system that needs "the current year" runs this lookup; it should be effectively free.                                                                                                               |
| Composite indexes       | See the constraint note below — this is the one place a composite _constraint_, not just an index, is the real recommendation.                                                                                                                            |
| Lookup tables           | None.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Enums                   | `status` (`ACTIVE`/`CLOSED`) — good native-enum candidate.                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Audit fields            | Full coverage — changing `promotionPolicy` mid-year, or which year is current, are both high-consequence, rare events that must be traceable to a specific admin action.                                                                                  |
| Soft delete             | No — academic years are never deleted, only closed (`status`). No `deletedAt` needed; `status = CLOSED` is the terminal state, and a closed year's data (enrollments, attendance, marks) must remain fully queryable forever.                             |
| History                 | `AcademicYear` _is_ the history axis every other year-scoped entity hangs off — it has no history of its own beyond its own row.                                                                                                                          |
| Archive strategy        | None — trivial row count (one per school per year; ~15 rows over the system's entire target lifespan for one school).                                                                                                                                     |
| Reporting impact        | The primary filter dimension for nearly every report — "this year's enrollment," "this year's attendance." Every reporting query should be written to accept an `academicYearId` parameter from day one, defaulting to current, not hardcoded to "today." |
| Expected query patterns | (a) "get the current year" (extremely hot path — arguably worth a request-scoped cache, see [PERFORMANCE_GUIDELINES.md § 4](./PERFORMANCE_GUIDELINES.md#4-caching-considerations)), (b) list years for historical reporting/TC generation.                |
| Expected growth         | ~1 row/year/school. Never a scale concern on its own.                                                                                                                                                                                                     |

**The one real constraint finding on this table:** [DOMAIN_MODEL.md § 3.6](../domain/DOMAIN_MODEL.md#36-academicyear) states "exactly one `isCurrent` at a time — business rule, not schema-enforced" and defers enforcement to "the mutation that sets it." As a database review, this deserves a direct answer: **it should be schema-enforced**, via a Postgres partial unique index —

```
CREATE UNIQUE INDEX one_current_year_per_school
  ON "AcademicYear" ("schoolId")
  WHERE "isCurrent" = true;
```

An application-only invariant is exactly the kind of rule that survives code review perfectly and then breaks under a concurrent request, a retried mutation, or a future engineer who doesn't know the rule exists and writes a second code path that sets `isCurrent`. A partial unique index makes the violation physically impossible regardless of which code path attempts it — the correct place for an invariant this cheap to enforce and this expensive to debug if silently violated. See [CONSTRAINT_STRATEGY.md § 3](./CONSTRAINT_STRATEGY.md#3-partial-unique-indexes) for the general pattern this is one instance of.

## 5. Section

| Dimension               | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary key             | `cuid2`.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Unique constraints      | **`(classId, academicYearId, name)` — missing from [DATABASE_SCHEMA.md § 3](../domain/DATABASE_SCHEMA.md#3-school--academic-structure).** Without it, nothing stops two "6-A" sections existing in the same year, which would silently corrupt roll-number uniqueness (`Enrollment`'s own `(sectionId, rollNumber)` unique wouldn't catch the duplicate-section case, only the duplicate-roll-number-within-one-section case). Flagged in [§ 8](#8-gaps-found-in-the-conceptual-model) as a correction to carry back to the domain model's schema. |
| Indexes                 | `(academicYearId, classId)` — "all sections for this class this year" is the roster-building query, run constantly by Admin/Teacher UIs.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Composite indexes       | Same as above; the unique constraint itself doubles as the composite index Postgres needs for this lookup pattern (unique indexes are queryable indexes too).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Lookup tables           | None.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Enums                   | None — `name` ("A", "B") is free text; schools vary in naming (some use numbers, some Greek-adjacent letters, rare cases use house/wing names).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Audit fields            | Standard coverage.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Soft delete             | Yes, `deletedAt` — a section can be discontinued (enrollment dropped) without losing the historical fact that students were once in it.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| History                 | Implicit via `Enrollment` rows pointing at it — `Section` itself is a reference entity, not a fact.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Archive strategy        | None needed — trivial row count (a handful per class per year).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Reporting impact        | Grouping dimension for nearly every class-level report (attendance summary, result summary).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Expected query patterns | (a) list sections for a class+year, (b) resolve a section's class (for `ClassSubject` lookups during marks-entry setup).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Expected growth         | Single-digit sections per class, ~9 classes (Nursery–8) → dozens of rows per school per year. Never a scale concern.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |

## 7. Teacher Assignment

| Dimension               | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary key             | `cuid2`.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Unique constraints      | `(academicYearId, sectionId, isClassTeacher)` scoped to `isClassTeacher = true` — a section has exactly one Class Teacher per year. This needs the **same partial-unique-index pattern** as `AcademicYear.isCurrent` (see [CONSTRAINT_STRATEGY.md § 3](./CONSTRAINT_STRATEGY.md#3-partial-unique-indexes)): `CREATE UNIQUE INDEX one_class_teacher_per_section ON "TeacherAssignment" ("sectionId", "academicYearId") WHERE "isClassTeacher" = true`. Subject-assignment rows (`isClassTeacher = false`) need their own uniqueness: `(academicYearId, sectionId, subjectId)` — one teacher per subject per section per year (a school could theoretically want co-teaching, but that's an unconfirmed future requirement, not something to design around speculatively; a single FK keeps the common case simple and a future `TeacherAssignment`-to-many-teachers redesign is a bounded, well-scoped future change if co-teaching is ever approved). |
| Indexes                 | `(teacherId, academicYearId)` — a teacher's own schedule/dashboard query, run on every login. **`(sectionId, academicYearId)` — the permission-check query, run on every single attendance and marks mutation** ("is this teacher allowed to touch this section"), making it one of the hottest-path lookups in the whole schema despite the table itself being small.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Composite indexes       | Both of the above are composite by nature; no further composites needed.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Lookup tables           | None.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Enums                   | None — `isClassTeacher` is a boolean, correctly so.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Audit fields            | Standard coverage — mid-year reassignment should be traceable, since it affects who could have legitimately marked a given historical attendance/marks record ([DOMAIN_MODEL.md § 6.2](../domain/DOMAIN_MODEL.md#62-teacherassignment)).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Soft delete             | Yes, `deletedAt` — reassignment removes access without destroying the historical fact "Teacher X taught Section Y in 2025–26," which `AttendanceRecord`/`MarksRecord` provenance depends on remaining answerable.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| History                 | The row itself, year-scoped, is the history — no separate history table needed as long as rows are never hard-deleted (they aren't; see [SOFT_DELETE_STRATEGY.md](./SOFT_DELETE_STRATEGY.md)).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Archive strategy        | None needed — bounded by `Teacher` count × subjects taught, trivially small even at 15-year scale.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Reporting impact        | Source for PTR (Pupil-Teacher Ratio) compliance reporting ([REPORTING_MODEL.md § 5](../domain/REPORTING_MODEL.md#5-future-compliance-reports)) — `COUNT(DISTINCT teacherId)` per school/year.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| Expected query patterns | (a) "what am I teaching" (teacher-scoped), (b) "who teaches this section" (section-scoped, permission-check), (c) "who is the class teacher for section X" (the partial-unique-backed lookup).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Expected growth         | ~Teacher count × 2–4 (subjects + possible class-teacher row) per year. Low hundreds of rows per school per year even at the large end of the target range.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |

## 8. Enrollment

| Dimension               | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary key             | **ULID or UUIDv7, not plain `cuid2`.** This is the join surface every high-volume child table (`AttendanceRecord`, `MarksRecord`) references — see [§ 11](#11-primary-key-strategy-summary) for why time-ordered IDs matter here even though `Enrollment` itself isn't high-volume: it's about keeping the _foreign key_ values in child tables roughly insertion-ordered, which helps those child tables' own index locality. Optional refinement, not a hard requirement — see the summary table for the exact recommendation. |
| Unique constraints      | `(studentId, academicYearId)` and `(sectionId, rollNumber)` — both already correct in [DATABASE_SCHEMA.md § 6](../domain/DATABASE_SCHEMA.md#6-enrollment--progression).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Indexes                 | Both unique constraints above already double as the two indexes that matter: student-history lookups (leading `studentId`) and section-roster lookups (leading `sectionId`). **No additional index needed** — a rare, worth-noting case of the constraints and the query patterns already lining up by construction.                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Composite indexes       | Covered above.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Lookup tables           | None.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Enums                   | None on `Enrollment` itself.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Audit fields            | Full coverage — mid-year section transfers and roll-number corrections are exactly the kind of change a parent dispute or an audit would need to trace precisely.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Soft delete             | **No delete mechanism of any kind, not even `deletedAt`.** Per [DOMAIN_MODEL.md § 6.1](../domain/DOMAIN_MODEL.md#61-enrollment), a promotion or withdrawal creates a new row or sets a status elsewhere (`PromotionRecord`) — it never removes an `Enrollment`. This is the physical enforcement of "history, not current state."                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| History                 | `Enrollment` **is** the history mechanism for every other year-scoped fact — this is the one entity in the whole schema whose entire purpose is being an immutable historical join surface.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Archive strategy        | None needed at V1/single-tenant scale — bounded by `Student` count × years enrolled, i.e., the same low-thousands scale as `Student` itself.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Reporting impact        | The join surface for essentially every report in [REPORTING_MODEL.md](../domain/REPORTING_MODEL.md) — Enrollment/School Strength, Attendance Summary, Examination Result Summary, Promotion/Detention Summary all pivot through it.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Expected query patterns | (a) "this student's enrollment this year" (exact match, `studentId` + `academicYearId`), (b) "this section's roster" (`sectionId`, used for attendance/marks rosters — this is the highest-frequency query against this table, run every time a teacher opens attendance or marks entry).                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Expected growth         | ~`Student` count per year (one row per active student per year) — the row count that matters is cumulative across years, not per-year: ~200–2,000 new rows/year/school, compounding to 3,000–30,000 rows over 15 years for one school. Meaningful but not partition-worthy on its own.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |

## 9. Attendance (`AttendanceSession` + `AttendanceRecord`)

| Dimension               | Recommendation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary key             | **ULID or UUIDv7 for `AttendanceRecord`** — this is the highest-insert-volume table in the entire schema (see growth estimate below), and it's inserted in large batches (a whole section's roster, ~20–40 rows) in a tight time window every school day. Random IDs (`cuid2`/UUIDv4) scatter these inserts across the B-tree; time-ordered IDs keep each day's batch physically adjacent, which matters materially once the table crosses low-millions of rows. `AttendanceSession` itself can stay `cuid2` (one row per section per day — trivial volume).                                                                                                                     |
| Unique constraints      | `AttendanceSession`: `(sectionId, date)` — already correct. `AttendanceRecord`: `(sessionId, enrollmentId)` — already correct.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Indexes                 | **`AttendanceRecord` needs an additional index on `(enrollmentId)` alone** — the existing unique constraint leads with `sessionId`, so "this student's attendance history for the year" (needed for TC generation, promotion-eligibility attendance-percentage checks, and the student attendance history report) would not use that index efficiently without a dedicated one.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Composite indexes       | `AttendanceSession`: `(schoolId, date)` for Admin's cross-section oversight view ("all sessions today, across every section").                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| Lookup tables           | None.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Enums                   | `AttendanceRecord.status` (`PRESENT`/`ABSENT`/`HALF_DAY`/`LEAVE`) — good native-enum candidate; [DOMAIN_MODEL.md § 7.2](../domain/DOMAIN_MODEL.md#72-attendancerecord) already states this is fixed, not school-configurable.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Audit fields            | Full coverage on `AttendanceRecord` edits — attendance percentage feeds promotion eligibility and TC content, so a silent retroactive edit is a real integrity risk, not just a nice-to-have trace.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Soft delete             | No delete mechanism on either table — corrections are audited edits (`AttendanceSession.lastEditedByUserId`/`lastEditedAt` already carry this), never row removal.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| History                 | The row itself, one per student per day, is the history. No separate history table.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Archive strategy        | **This is the table that will eventually need one.** See growth estimate — worth partitioning `AttendanceRecord` by `academicYearId` (or a derived date range) from the _first_ migration that creates it, even though V1 volume doesn't demand it yet. Partitioning an empty or near-empty table costs nothing; partitioning a 2-million-row table later requires either a maintenance window or an online-partitioning tool most teams don't have on hand. See [AUDIT_STRATEGY.md § 3](./AUDIT_STRATEGY.md#3-partitioning) for the same argument applied to `AuditLog`, and [PERFORMANCE_GUIDELINES.md § 2](./PERFORMANCE_GUIDELINES.md#2-growth-projections) for the numbers. |
| Reporting impact        | Source for Attendance Summary and Student Attendance History ([REPORTING_MODEL.md § 2](../domain/REPORTING_MODEL.md#2-attendance-reports)); feeds `HALF_DAY`-weighted percentage calculations that must read the school's configured weight ([BUSINESS_RULES.md § 4](../domain/BUSINESS_RULES.md#4-attendance)), not a hardcoded 0.5 — a reporting-query concern, not a schema one, but worth restating here since it directly affects how the aggregation query must be written.                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Expected query patterns | (a) "has today's session been marked" (exact `sectionId`+`date` match — the unique index), (b) "this student's attendance this year" (`enrollmentId`-scoped, needs the added index above), (c) cross-section date-range aggregation for reports.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Expected growth         | **The single largest table in the schema.** ~200 school days/year × student count → 40,000–400,000 rows/year for one school (200–2,000 students). Over 15 years, one school alone: **600,000–6,000,000 rows.** At real multi-school scale (Epic H), this is the table that determines whether partitioning was a good decision made early or a painful one made late.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |

## 11. Primary Key Strategy — Summary

The domain model's illustrative schema used `cuid()` (Prisma's default) uniformly. Reviewed as a whole, not per-table:

| Table                                                                                                                                | Recommendation                     | Why                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| `AttendanceRecord`, `MarksRecord`, `AuditLog`                                                                                        | **ULID or UUIDv7**                 | High insert volume, batch-inserted in tight time windows, B-tree locality genuinely matters at the row counts § 9–10 project.                                                                                                                                                                    |
| `Enrollment`                                                                                                                         | ULID/UUIDv7 optional, not required | Not itself high-volume, but is the FK target of the three tables above — insertion-ordered `Enrollment` IDs give those child tables' foreign-key columns better locality too. A reasonable "if the team is already adopting ULIDs elsewhere, extend it here" call, not a standalone requirement. |
| Everything else (`Student`, `Teacher`, `Guardian`, `Section`, `Subject`, `Class`, `AcademicYear`, `School`, reference/config tables) | `cuid2` (Prisma default)           | Low insert volume; ID ordering has no measurable effect on these tables' performance. Using `cuid2` uniformly here keeps the schema simple where the complexity of a second ID scheme buys nothing.                                                                                              |

This is a genuinely narrow, targeted recommendation — three (or four) tables out of ~29, not a blanket schema-wide change. Applying ULIDs everywhere "for consistency" would be exactly the kind of uniform-but-unjustified decision this project's YAGNI culture already rejects elsewhere; applying it nowhere would leave real, measurable performance on the table for the three tables that will actually accumulate millions of rows.

## 14. The Four Questions, Answered Directly

### Why should `AttendanceRecord` reference `Enrollment` instead of `Student`?

Because attendance is inherently a fact about a student's placement _in a specific year_, not about the student's eternal identity. A student detained and repeating a class has two `Enrollment`s in the same `Class` across two different years — if `AttendanceRecord` referenced `Student` directly, every query would need to additionally disambiguate _which year_ by joining through a date range (fragile, and `Enrollment` doesn't carry explicit start/end dates — only an `academicYearId`), instead of a single, unambiguous foreign key. Referencing `Enrollment` also makes the two highest-value queries against this table — "this student's attendance this year" and "this section's roster today" — both naturally year-scoped and section-scoped by construction, with no risk of accidentally aggregating attendance across a student's multiple, unrelated years in the same class.

### Why should Results (`MarksRecord`) reference `Enrollment`?

The same reasoning, with a sharper failure mode if ignored: a report card is supposed to reflect _this year's_ performance. If `MarksRecord` referenced `Student` directly, a detained student's two years of marks in the same `Class` would have no clean way to stay separated at the schema level — the application would have to filter by exam date ranges to reconstruct which year a mark belonged to, and a bug in that filtering logic would silently blend two different years' results into one report card. Referencing `Enrollment` makes that blending structurally impossible: `MarksRecord.enrollmentId` unambiguously identifies which year's placement a mark belongs to, and the `(enrollmentId, examSubjectScheduleId)` uniqueness constraint means a student can't accidentally receive two marks for what look like "the same" exam across two different years, because they're two different `Enrollment`s with two different `Examination` rows (each `Examination` is itself year-scoped via `ExamTerm.academicYearId`).

### Which tables should be append-only?

Strictly (no `UPDATE`, no `DELETE`, ever, after creation): **`AuditLog`**, **`TransferCertificate`**, and — per the refinement in § 13 — **`PromotionRecord`**. `ReportCard` is append-only _per version_ (each generation is a new row; nothing is ever overwritten, though nothing is ever deleted either). `AttendanceRecord` and `MarksRecord` are **not** strictly append-only — they're mutable-with-full-audit-trail, a deliberate, considered choice (see [AUDIT_STRATEGY.md § 4](./AUDIT_STRATEGY.md#4-why-attendancerecordmarksrecord-are-mutable-not-event-sourced)) rather than an oversight: true event-sourcing for every attendance mark would be disproportionate complexity for this system's actual scale and requirements, and the audit trail already provides equivalent traceability without it.

### Which entities should never be updated?

`TransferCertificate` (immutable from issuance), `PromotionRecord` (immutable from decision, per the § 13 refinement), `ReportCard` (immutable per version — a "correction" is a new row), and `AuditLog` rows themselves (an audit entry's `beforeValue`/`afterValue` must never change after being written, or the log stops being trustworthy as a log).

### Which entities should never be deleted?

Two distinct answers depending on what "deleted" means. **Never soft-deleted either** (no `deletedAt`, no status-based removal, not even by Admin): `Enrollment`, `AttendanceRecord`, `MarksRecord`, `PromotionRecord`, `TransferCertificate`, `ReportCard`, `AuditLog` — every entity that represents a historical fact or measurement rather than a current-state reference. **Soft-deletable, but never hard-deleted**: `Student`, `Teacher`, `Guardian`, `Class`, `Section`, `Subject`, `TeacherAssignment`, and the rest of the reference/administrative entities — these can be deactivated (`status`/`deletedAt`) but a hard `DELETE` should never be a code path the application exposes, because every one of them is referenced by historical facts that must remain resolvable (a soft-deleted `Teacher` must still be identifiable as the person who marked a 2024 attendance record).

## 15. Gaps Found in the Conceptual Model

Three physical omissions in [docs/domain/DATABASE_SCHEMA.md](../domain/DATABASE_SCHEMA.md), found while reviewing for constraints — not conceptual disagreements, straightforward corrections to carry back into that document on its own next edit:

1. **`Section` has no `@@unique([classId, academicYearId, name])`** — see § 5.
2. **`Subject` has no `@@unique([schoolId, name])`** — see § 6.
3. **`TransferCertificate.tcNumber` is drafted `@unique` (globally) instead of `@@unique([schoolId, tcNumber])`** — see § 12. This is the highest-priority of the three: it's the one gap that would actively break at the moment a second tenant is onboarded (Epic H), not just a missing data-integrity guard for the single-tenant case the other two are.
